Problem 1 Error analysis for the midpoint rule

To do the error analysis, let us perform the Taylor expansion: \begin{align*} f(x) & =f(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+(x-x_{j}-\frac{h}{2})f'(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+\frac{1}{2}(x-x_{j}-\frac{h}{2})^{2}f''(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+\dots\\ & =f(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+(x-x_{j}-\frac{h}{2})f'(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+\frac{1}{2}(x-x_{j}-\frac{h}{2})^{2}f''(\xi_{j}) \end{align*} where \xi_{j} is a point between x and x_{j}+\frac{h}{2}. With this, we can write \int_{x_{j}}^{x_{j+1}}f(x)\,dx=\int_{x_{j}}^{x_{j+1}}dx\,\left[f(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+(x-x_{j}-\frac{h}{2})f'(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+\frac{1}{2}(x-x_{j}-\frac{h}{2})^{2}f''(\xi_{j})\right] Let us define u=\frac{x-x_{j}-h/2}{h}\quad\Rightarrow\quad du=dx/h. We have \begin{align*} \int_{x_{j}}^{x_{j+1}}f(x)\,dx & =\int_{-1/2}^{1/2}du\left[hf(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+h^{2}uf'(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+\frac{h^{3}}{2}u^{2}f''(\xi_{j})\right]\\ & =hf(x_{j}+\frac{h}{2})+\frac{h^{3}}{24}f''(\xi_{j}). \end{align*} Thus, the single-panel error is \mathcal{E}_j = \frac{h^3}{24}f''(\xi_j).

The the composite version error \mathcal{E} = \sum_{j=0}^{n-1}\frac{h^3}{24}f''(\xi_j) = \frac{(b-a)h^2}{24}\overline{f''}.

First, notice that (x_{i}-x_{i+1})=-h, (x_{i}-x_{i+2})=-2h, (x_{i+1}-x_{i+2})=-h. Calculating the following integral using Monte Carlo method: I = \int_{x^2+y^2\leq 1} dxdy\, e^{-(x^2+y^2)}x^2

Let us first work out it analytically. \begin{align*} \frac{\int_{x^{2}+y^{2}\leq1}dxdy\,\exp(-(x^{2}+y^{2}))x^{2}}{\int_{x^{2}+y^{2}\leq1}dxdy\,\exp(-(x^{2}+y^{2}))} & =\frac{\int_{0}^{1}rdr\int_{0}^{2\pi}d\theta\exp(-r^{2})r^{2}\cos^{2}\theta}{2\pi\int_{0}^{1}rdr\exp(-r^{2})}\\ & =\frac{\int_{0}^{1}dr\,r^{3}\exp(-r^{2})\int_{0}^{2\pi}d\theta\,\cos^{2}\theta}{\pi(1-1/e)}\\ & =\frac{\int_{0}^{1}dr\,r\exp(-r)}{2(1-1/e)}\\ & =\frac{(e-2)}{e}\times\frac{e}{2(e-1)}\\ & =\frac{e-2}{2(e-1)}\\ & \simeq 0.21 \end{align*}
